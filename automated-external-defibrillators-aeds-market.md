<p><p>Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs) come in two main types: semi-automated and fully automated. Semi-automated AEDs require the user to push a button to deliver a shock, while fully automated AEDs deliver a shock automatically without any user intervention. Both types are designed to quickly and effectively treat sudden cardiac arrest by restoring a normal heart rhythm. The choice between semi-automated and fully automated AEDs depends on the user's comfort level with administering the shock and the level of automation desired.</p></p>
