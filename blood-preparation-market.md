<p><ul><li>Whole Blood</li><li>Blood Components</li><li>Blood Derivatives</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Blood Preparation Market includes three main types: Whole Blood, Blood Components, and Blood Derivatives. Whole Blood refers to blood collected from donors, which is used for transfusions as a complete entity without any modification. Blood Components are obtained from Whole Blood through a process called fractionation, which separates the different components like red cells, plasma, and platelets. Blood Derivatives are further processed versions of Blood Components, such as albumin, immunoglobulins, and clotting factors, which are extracted and purified for specific therapeutic purposes.</p></p>
