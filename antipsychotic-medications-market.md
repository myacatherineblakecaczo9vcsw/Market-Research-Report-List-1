<p><p>The antipsychotic medications market is categorized into various types based on their mechanism of action. Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs) work by increasing serotonin levels in the brain, while Serotonin Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors (SNRIs) target both serotonin and norepinephrine. Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs) inhibit the reuptake of serotonin and norepinephrine, and Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors (MAOIs) block the action of enzymes that break down neurotransmitters. Reversible Inhibitors of Monoamine Oxidase-A (RIMA) selectively inhibit the enzyme monoamine oxidase-A. Tetracyclic Antidepressants (TeCAs) and Noradrenergic and Specific Serotonergic Antidepressant (NaSSAs) target specific receptors to achieve their intended effects.</p></p>
