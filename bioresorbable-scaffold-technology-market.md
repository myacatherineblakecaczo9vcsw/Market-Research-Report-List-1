<p><p>Bioresorbable Scaffold Technology refers to temporary implants used in medical procedures that gradually dissolve in the body over time. The two main types of bioresorbable scaffold materials are metal and polymer. Metal bioresorbable scaffolds are typically made of materials like magnesium or iron, while polymer bioresorbable scaffolds are made of materials like polylactic acid or polyglycolic acid. Both types of scaffolds provide temporary support to tissues and organs before being absorbed by the body, reducing the risk of long-term complications.</p></p>
