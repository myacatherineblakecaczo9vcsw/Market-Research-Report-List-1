<p><p>The market for 3D printed organs is comprised of two main types: hydrogel and living cells. Hydrogel-based organs are created using a bioink made of water and synthetic polymers, which provides a scaffold for cells to grow into functional tissues. Living cell-based organs, on the other hand, are created by printing layers of living cells directly onto a scaffold, allowing for the formation of functional organs. Both types of technology hold great potential for revolutionizing the healthcare industry by providing personalized, on-demand organ transplants.</p></p>
