<p><p>Microactuators are devices that convert electrical energy into mechanical motion on a micro-scale. There are several types of microactuators on the market, including electrostatic, which use electric fields to generate movement; electromagnetic, which rely on magnetic fields; piezoelectric, which utilize the piezoelectric effect to produce motion; fluid microactuators, which use liquids or gases to drive motion; and thermal microactuators, which operate by harnessing heat. Each type of microactuator has unique characteristics and applications in various industries such as robotics, biomedical devices, and automotive systems.</p></p>
