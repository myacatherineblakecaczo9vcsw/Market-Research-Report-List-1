<p><p>Small-scale LNG refers to liquefied natural gas projects that have a smaller capacity compared to large-scale projects. The market types involved in small-scale LNG can be divided into two categories: liquefaction terminals and regasification terminals. Liquefaction terminals are plants where natural gas is cooled and transformed into LNG for storage and transportation. Regasification terminals, on the other hand, are facilities where LNG is converted back into its gaseous state for distribution and use. These two market types play crucial roles in expanding the use of LNG in various industries and regions.</p></p>
