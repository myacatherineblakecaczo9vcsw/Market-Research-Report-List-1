<p><p>The permanent magnets market is segmented into two main types: ferrite magnets and rare earth magnets. Ferrite magnets, also known as ceramic magnets, are widely used due to their low cost and good resistance to corrosion. On the other hand, rare earth magnets, which include neodymium and samarium-cobalt magnets, offer much stronger magnetic fields but come at a higher cost. Rare earth magnets are essential in many high-tech applications such as electric vehicles, wind turbines, and electronics.</p></p>
