<p><strong>The Midwives Service Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Certified Nurse Midwives (CNMs)</li><li>Direct Entry Midwives (DEMs)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Certified Nurse Midwives (CNMs) are registered nurses who have completed advanced education in midwifery. They provide care to women during pregnancy, childbirth, and postpartum, often in hospitals and under the supervision of doctors. CNMs are well-equipped to handle high-risk pregnancies and medical interventions.</p><p>On the other hand, Direct Entry Midwives (DEMs) belong to a different market segment. They acquire midwifery expertise through non-nursing pathways, such as apprenticeships or specific midwifery programs. DEMs primarily emphasize natural childbirth and homebirths, but some also work in birth centers or collaborate with healthcare professionals.</p><p>Both CNMs and DEMs play essential roles in providing specialized care to pregnant women, yet their practices and training paths differ, addressing distinct market demands.</p></p>
