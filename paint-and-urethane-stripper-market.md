<p><p>Paint and urethane strippers can be categorized into two main types based on their chemical composition and method of action: alkaline and acidic. Alkaline paint strippers work by breaking down the bonds in paint and urethane through a high-pH solution, making it easier to remove. On the other hand, acidic paint strippers contain chemicals that dissolve the paint and urethane coatings on the surface. Both types have their own advantages and disadvantages, depending on the specific application and substrate being worked on.</p></p>
