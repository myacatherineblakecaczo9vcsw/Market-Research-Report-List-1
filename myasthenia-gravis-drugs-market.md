<p><p>The Myasthenia Gravis Drugs market includes three main types of medications. Anticholinesterases, such as pyridostigmine, work by increasing the levels of a chemical messenger that helps improve muscle strength and function. Immunosuppressants, like azathioprine or mycophenolate mofetil, reduce the activity of the immune system, which helps decrease the attack on muscle cells. Intravenous immune globulins, known as IVIG, are made from donated blood and contain healthy antibodies that can block the harmful ones responsible for damaging the muscle cells. These medications aim to manage the symptoms and improve the quality of life for patients with Myasthenia Gravis.</p></p>
