<p><p>Organic photochromic materials are sensitive to light and can change color or opacity when exposed to ultraviolet or visible light. Naphthopyrans, diarylethenes, azobenzenes, spiropyrans, and hexaarylbiimidazoles are some common types of organic photochromic materials used in various applications. Naphthopyrans exhibit reversible color changes, while diarylethenes have high fatigue resistance. Azobenzenes show light-induced isomerization, spiropyrans exhibit rapid color change, and hexaarylbiimidazoles are known for their high photochromic efficiency. These materials find applications in eyewear, sensors, data storage, and security features.</p></p>
