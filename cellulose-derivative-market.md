<p><p>Cellulose derivatives can be broadly classified into three main types: cellulose ether, cellulose ester, and cellulose ether and ester. Cellulose ether is a water-soluble derivative often used in pharmaceuticals, construction, and personal care products. Cellulose ester, on the other hand, is a thermoplastic material commonly found in films, coatings, and adhesives. Cellulose ether and ester, as the name suggests, combines the properties of both derivatives, making it versatile for various applications in industries such as food, textiles, and paper.</p></p>
